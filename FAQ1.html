Frequently Asked Questions

Mental health refers to a person's emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act.
Common signs include:
  • Persistent sadness or mood changes
  • Excessive fear, worry, or anxiety
  • Social withdrawal or isolation
  • Changes in sleeping or eating habits
Common types include:
  • Anxiety disorders
  • Mood disorders (e.g., depression, bipolar disorder)
  • Personality disorders
  • Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

Seek help if:
  • You feel overwhelmed by emotions that don't go away
  • You are struggling to function in your daily life
  • Thoughts of self-harm or suicide arise
Managing stress and anxiety can include:
  • Practice relaxation techniques like deep breathing
  • Exercise regularly
  • Maintain a balanced diet and proper sleep
  • Talk to a therapist or counselor
Therapy provides a safe space to explore thoughts and feelings, learn coping strategies, and work toward personal growth. It may involve talking about difficult experiences, setting goals, and receiving guidance from a mental health professional.
While many mental health conditions cannot be "cured" in the traditional sense, they can often be managed effectively with treatment. This can include therapy, medication, lifestyle changes, or a combination of these approaches.
